Model1 Issues

I have 2 problems with my Model 1 mixer, which is still under warranty.

  1. DSUBs inputs thread is stripped
  2. Filter pop has very audible artifacts

DSUBs Inputs

The thread on my DSUBs inputs stripped after being used twice. You will see in the video below that when I put the DSUB cable into the 1st input the bolts go in all the way without any resistance. When I put the same cable in the DSUB output, there is resistance & the bolt works as expected (it screws on).

I tried this same cable on the DSUBs input of the display unit at SuperBooth 2023 yesterday & it worked as expected. So I know it's not the cable.

Master Filter Pop/Click

I can hear very audible artifacts when toggling the master filter. I know the Model 1 advertises its "zero-crossing detection system", which is meant to minimize artifacts when toggling the channel & master filters.

I was told by Allen&Heath support that this does not guarantee the toggling will be completely without artifacts. I've compared my mixer to 2 other Model 1 mixer units & the other two are either completely silent or the artifacts are barely audible (significantly quieter than on mine). I also spoke with one of the Play Differently ambassadors who said that in his experience the master filter toggle is always silent.

I would like you to hear how the toggling sounds on my mixer & confirm if you think this is normal (in comparison to ther Model 1 units).

In the video below you can hear & see the audible artifacts on mine. The ridge in the spectrogram is the filter toggle.

You need to be listening with headphones or on proper speakers to hear this.

Instructions to reproduce:

  1. Turn on master filter on left side.
  2. Reduce some of the high frequencies by twisting the LPF knob to the left.
  3. Toggle the master filter for a specific channel while there is sound going through that channel.

The pop/click is easier to hear when you are playing a bass sound, but you can play any type of sound & still hear it.
